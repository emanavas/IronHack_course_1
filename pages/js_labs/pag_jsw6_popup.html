History of Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize in Physics. She was born in Warsaw, Poland, in 1867 and moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne. Curie discovered two elements, polonium and radium, and her work laid the foundation for advances in medical treatment and nuclear physics. She died in 1934 from aplastic anemia, likely caused by her prolonged exposure to radiation. Curie's legacy continues to inspire scientists. She is remembered for her groundbreaking contributions to science and her role as a trailblazer for women in science. Her work has had a lasting impact on the fields of physics and chemistry, and she remains an iconic figure in the history of science.
